Automatic Video Object Removal

VGGT4D-initiated SAM3 mask propagation and DiffuEraser inpainting — project page with qualitative mask_vis and quantitative summaries on DAVIS.

Abstract

Video object removal requires temporally stable segmentation of a target region together with plausible inpainting of the occluded background. Strong video segmenters typically assume explicit user interaction on the first frame, while geometry-aware motion cues can propose foreground-like regions automatically but often yield fragmented masks or weak alignment with object boundaries when a single salient instance must be removed.

This project studies a fully automatic pipeline that couples VGGT4D dynamic-region cues with SAM3 mask propagation (including a two-stage automatic anchoring merge when applicable) and DiffuEraser diffusion-based inpainting. VGGT4D produces per-frame dynamic masks; they are fused into an initialization that prompts SAM3 for refined instance masks over time; DiffuEraser then inpaints conditioned on the mask sequence. Compared with feeding raw VGGT-style dynamic masks directly to the inpainter, SAM3 refinement improves single-object masks and temporal stability; compared with interactive-only setups, the pipeline derives prompts from motion priors without manual clicks or boxes.
